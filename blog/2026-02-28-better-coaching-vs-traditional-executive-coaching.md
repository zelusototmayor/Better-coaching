Executive coaching has traditionally been out of reach for most professionals.

The standard model: $500–$1,000 per session, monthly or bi-weekly meetings, a 6–12 month engagement. Total cost: $6,000–$24,000 per year. This is why coaching has historically been a C-suite perk — a benefit reserved for senior leaders whose development the organization is willing to fund substantially.

Better Coaching is built on a different premise: the professionals who most need structured development — new managers, individual contributors with leadership ambitions, executives who want consistent accountability — often can't access the traditional model, either because of cost or because monthly sessions aren't frequent enough to drive real change.

This comparison is intended to be honest. Traditional executive coaching is genuinely excellent for certain things. Better Coaching delivers something different, and knowing which fits your situation is more useful than a one-sided sales pitch.

## What Traditional Executive Coaching Is

Traditional executive coaching typically means a 1:1 engagement with a credentialed human coach. Standard features:

- **Frequency:** Monthly or bi-weekly sessions (1–2 hours each)
- **Format:** Structured conversation — goal-setting, reflection, accountability
- **Duration:** 6–12 month engagements (most coaching relationships)
- **Cost:** $300–$1,500 per session, depending on coach seniority and market
- **Access:** Appointment-based; your coach is available during sessions, not on demand
- **Relationship:** Long-term, personalized — the coach builds deep knowledge of your context over months

At its best, traditional executive coaching is transformational. A skilled coach who works with you over 12 months — who knows your history, your relationships, your patterns — can help you see things about yourself that no other tool can surface.

The limitations are structural: cost, cadence, and access.

## What Better Coaching Is

Better Coaching is an accountability and development platform built for consistent weekly practice, not monthly conversation.

- **Format:** Guided reflection, goal tracking, skill development frameworks, and structured accountability — at any time
- **Cost:** A fraction of traditional coaching — accessible without organizational sponsorship
- **Access:** Available when you need it — not when a session happens to be scheduled

Better Coaching doesn't replace the human depth that the best executive coaches provide. It delivers something different: consistent structure, regular accountability, and development support that works between sessions — or instead of them.

## Where Traditional Coaching Is Better

Be honest about this: traditional executive coaching has genuine advantages in certain situations.

**When the relationship is everything**  
The best executive coaches are exceptional at reading between the lines — noticing what you're *not* saying, asking the question that reframes a problem entirely, and building the kind of trust where you're willing to say things you've never said out loud. This requires a skilled human who knows you well. No platform fully replicates it.

**When organizational politics are the core issue**  
Navigating a difficult board, managing a fractured leadership team, or positioning yourself in a complex organization — these are deeply human situations where a coach's external perspective and political intelligence are genuinely valuable. This is high-stakes, high-context coaching that benefits from a senior human expert.

**When the stakes are highest**  
If you're a CEO preparing for a major acquisition, a VP preparing for a board presentation, or a founder navigating a crisis — the highest-stakes moments often benefit from the most senior, most experienced coaching available. In those moments, cost is less relevant than expertise.

**The honest answer:** If you can access traditional executive coaching with a genuinely excellent coach, at a frequency and cost that works for your situation — do it. It's valuable.

## Where Better Coaching Is Better

**Consistency that monthly sessions can't provide**  
Development happens between sessions, not during them. A 1-hour conversation every month creates a spike of clarity — and then 29 days of drift back to old patterns. Better Coaching's daily accountability structure catches the drift before it compounds.

Research consistently shows that behavior change requires frequency, not intensity. A 15-minute daily reflection with accountability prompts drives more measurable change than an intensive monthly session.

**The people traditional coaching doesn't reach**  
Most new managers, team leads, and high-potential individual contributors will never receive traditional executive coaching. Their organizations won't fund it, and $600–$1,000/month isn't personally justifiable. Better Coaching makes structured professional development accessible to the people who most need it.

**The between-sessions gap**  
Even professionals who do work with an executive coach often want something between sessions — a place to log what happened, track commitments, process a difficult situation, and maintain momentum. Better Coaching works as a standalone development platform and as a complement to existing coaching relationships.

**Speed to start**  
Traditional coaching involves a discovery process, matching, contracting, and a ramp-up period. Better Coaching starts working from day one — structured, goal-oriented, and contextually aware from the first session.

**Specific skill development at scale**  
Traditional coaching is conversation-based; it's exceptional at insight but not always structured for skill repetition. Better Coaching provides frameworks for the specific skills that matter: feedback delivery, 1:1 conversations, difficult decisions, performance management. These aren't just discussed — they're practiced with structure.
