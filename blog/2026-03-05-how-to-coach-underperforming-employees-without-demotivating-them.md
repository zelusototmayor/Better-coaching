Underperformance is rarely fixed by one hard conversation. It improves through clear diagnosis, specific expectations, and consistent coaching follow-through.

## The manager mistake that makes things worse

Most managers start with a judgment statement: "You're not meeting expectations." That triggers defensiveness and shuts down honest problem-solving.

Start with observable facts instead:

- Missed deadlines in 3 of the last 4 cycles
- Handoffs requiring rework from peers
- Low output in highest-priority tasks

Facts reduce emotion and make improvement plans concrete.

## The 4-step coaching structure

1. **Clarify the gap** (current vs expected behavior)
2. **Diagnose causes** (skill, clarity, capacity, motivation, or process)
3. **Set 2-week improvement targets** (measurable and realistic)
4. **Run weekly coaching check-ins** focused on obstacles + support

## Example target format

- Deliver draft by Tuesday 12:00 on 3 consecutive weeks
- Reduce QA corrections from 7 to 2 or fewer per sprint
- Send stakeholder updates every Friday before 17:00

## Coaching language that works

Use: "Let's define what success looks like for the next two weeks."

Avoid: "You need to step up."

Specificity improves accountability and preserves trust.

## Where AI coaching helps managers

Managers can use AI coaching tools to rehearse difficult conversations, get feedback on tone, and tighten question quality before live meetings. This reduces avoidance and improves consistency across the team.

## Closing

Performance improves when people know exactly what to change, by when, and how support will be provided. Coaching is not soft accountability — it's structured accountability.
